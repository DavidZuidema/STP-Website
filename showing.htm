Telling vs. Showing

Wouldn't it would be much easier if people made buying decisions with a purely logical and rational mindset? The reality is that our emotions are more powerful than we sometimes give them credit for. How we feel has a significant impact when we are deciding to buy.

Telling is too direct. We've all been bombarded for too long by all kinds of direct, left-brain focused messages. The result: everyone is now automatically skeptical and mistrusting of most marketing messages. We've developed spam filters for unwanted marketing.

But there's good news. While your competitors continue to spam people with messages that no one trusts, you can use the humor and drama of a story to reach people indirectly, by appealing to their emotions. A story tells us about how the characters feel, what they experience, and how they make decisions. The story doesn't get caught in the spam filter because its talking about some other character, not us directly. But, as the story progresses, we naturally slide ourselves into those same characters' shoes, feel what they feel, and decide what we would do in that situation. When Rich tells the story about Leo's first shippment at Accuri, you see things from Leo's perspective and start to understand on a much deeper level why working with Menlo is truely unique. If Rich had come right out and told you that Menlo is awesome, it wouldn't have had the same effect.

Adding a video to your website is a great, low-risk way for your prospective customers to go beyond a list of features 'n benefits and actually test drive a story about how the product fits into their lives. A video allows you to show your customers what you can do for them, without having to just tell them how great you are.

Allowing people to experience your product/service, without having to buy it first, is the best way to lower the barrier to entry for your prospective customers.
